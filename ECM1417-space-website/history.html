Launch of the First Artificial Satellite (Sputnik 1, 1957)

Sputnik 1 became the first human‑made object to orbit Earth, marking the beginning of the space age and igniting the global space race.

First Human‑Made Object to Reach the Moon (Luna 2, 1959)

Luna 2 became the first spacecraft to reach the Moon, proving that deep‑space travel and interplanetary exploration were possible.

First Human in Space (1961)

Yuri Gagarin became the first human to journey into outer space, completing an orbit of Earth aboard Vostok 1 and marking the beginning of human spaceflight.

Apollo 11 Moon Landing (1969)

NASA’s Apollo 11 mission successfully landed humans on the Moon for the first time. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above.

International Space Station (1998–Present)

The ISS is one of the greatest international collaborations in history, serving as a microgravity laboratory for scientific research and long‑duration human spaceflight.

Recent Mars Exploration Missions

Rovers such as Curiosity and Perseverance, along with orbiters and landers, have expanded our understanding of Mars and paved the way for future human missions.
